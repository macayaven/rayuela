CRITICAL RULES:
1. Strip ALL editorial apparatus: tables of contents, copyright pages, edition notes, publisher blurbs, digital navigation markers, page numbers, running headers, translator notes, "About the Author" sections.
2. Do NOT strip: author's epigraphs, fictional footnotes (e.g., in Borges, Bolaño), embedded poems/songs, typographic experiments, intentional formatting, chapter/story titles written by the author.
3. "Chapter N" / "Capítulo N" / Roman numeral headers at the start of chapters: STRIP these (they are editorial formatting, not prose). But preserve them in the number field.
4. For STORY COLLECTIONS (Borges, Quiroga): each story = one "chapter" entry. Preserve story titles in the title field.
5. For NOVELS WITH PARTS (e.g., Cien años by decade, Tres tristes tigres by section): record the part/section name in the section field.
6. For NOVELS WITHOUT EXPLICIT CHAPTER BREAKS: segment by the natural structural divisions the author intended (e.g., paragraph breaks that signal scene changes, typographic markers like "* * *"). Document your segmentation logic clearly in the preprocessing log.
7. Preserve all accent marks (á, é, í, ó, ú), ñ, ü, ¿, ¡, and special characters exactly as they appear.
8. If text appears corrupted or garbled, FLAG it in the preprocessing log — do not silently fix or omit it.
9. Be paranoid about data hygiene. When in doubt, strip it and document what you removed.
